# Competitive Pricing Analysis — SeedLink Bundle Deals

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Prepared for:** Shilpa Kollengode, CEO
**Analyst:** Veteran Vectors

---

## Executive Summary

**Verdict: Yes, you are charging too little — significantly so for the Content Package and Full Stack bundles.**

SeedLink's one-time build pricing sits at 50–80% below what comparable services cost on a *monthly* basis. The Outreach Package ($2,500) is competitively priced for a market-entry play. The Content Package ($4,000) and Full Stack ($6,000) are materially underpriced relative to the value delivered and the competitive landscape.

The pricing works as a land-and-expand strategy, but it leaves $3,000–$8,000+ on the table per deal — money that competitors are capturing monthly.

The Full Stack package ($6,000) includes:
- 7+ n8n workflows (content pipeline, social derivation, scheduling, editorial calendar, SEO optimizer, analytics, outreach handler)

**Estimated labor: 120–200+ hours across all modules.**

## 6. Recommendations

### A. Raise the Content Package to $5,500–$6,500

The Content Package (Content Creation + SEO/GEO) is the most labor-intensive build and delivers the highest ongoing value. At $4,000 it's priced at the *freelancer floor*. A $5,500–$6,500 price:
- Still saves clients 80%+ vs. agency retainers
- Reflects 50–70 hours of skilled work
- Maintains the "easy yes" value story
- Improves net effective rate from ~$35/hr to ~$50/hr

### B. Raise the Full Stack to $8,000–$10,000

The Full Stack bundle ($6,000) is the biggest undercharge. At 120–200 hours of work, $6,000 yields $30–$50/hr *before* the SeedLink commission split. Recommendation:
- **$8,500** as new anchor price
- Still dramatically below the $15,000–$50,000 agency range
- Value story: "Replace $60K+/year in agency costs with an $8,500 one-time build"
- Net effective rate improves to $45–$70/hr

### C. Keep Outreach at $2,500 (For Now)

The Outreach Package has the best margin-to-effort ratio and the most templated, repeatable process. At $2,500 it's a strong market-entry offer. Consider raising to $3,000 after the first 10 builds.

### D. Consider Tiered Pricing Within Packages

Instead of flat pricing, introduce Good/Better/Best within each package:

| Package | Standard | Enhanced | Premium |
|---------|----------|----------|---------|
| **Outreach** | $2,500 (1 account) | $3,500 (2 accounts) | $5,000 (3 accounts + CRM) |
| **Content** | $5,500 (1 pillar, 4 posts/mo) | $7,500 (2 pillars, 8 posts/mo) | $10,000 (3 pillars, 12 posts/mo) |
| **Full Stack** | $8,500 (basic all-4) | $11,000 (enhanced) | $14,000 (premium) |

### E. Reframe Lite Support as Ongoing ROI

At $250–$350/mo, Lite Support is 90% cheaper than agency retainers. But it's currently positioned as "maintenance." Reframe it as:
- "Managed AI Operations" — $500–$750/mo
- Include monthly performance review, prompt optimization, and content strategy adjustment
- This is where recurring revenue compounds

### F. Add a "Quick Win" Assessment at $750–$1,000

Already recommended in the SMB analysis. A paid assessment creates a low-risk entry that:
- Validates client fit before committing to a build
- Generates revenue even if the client doesn't proceed
- Creates urgency to upgrade to the full build

## 8. Bottom Line

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Are we charging too little? | **Yes — especially on Content and Full Stack packages** |
| How much too little? | **30–60% below mid-market agency rates** |
| Will raising prices lose deals? | **Unlikely** — even at $8,500 Full Stack, the value story is overwhelming (replace $60K+/yr agency spend) |
| Where is pricing OK? | **Outreach Package ($2,500)** and **SaaS tiers ($197–$697/mo)** |
| Biggest revenue leak? | **Lite Support at $250–$350/mo** — should be $500–$750/mo as "Managed AI Operations" |
| Immediate action? | Raise Content to $5,500–$6,500, Full Stack to $8,500, reframe Lite Support |
