# Revenue Share vs. Build-and-Give: Why VV Should Retain a Revenue Share on the White-Label Platform

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Prepared for:** Internal Strategy (Veteran Vectors)
**Classification:** Confidential

---

## Executive Summary

A one-time build fee caps VV's total compensation at $37K–$65K regardless of platform success. A 35–40% revenue share on even modest traction (50 subscribers at $397/mo blended avg) generates $89K+/year — every year, with compounding upside. The math is unambiguous: build-and-give is a structurally poor deal for the party that creates 100% of the sellable product.

**Recommendation: Option B (discounted build fee + 35–40% revenue share) is the clear winner.** The fee discount is recovered in ~4 months at 50 subscribers. After month 4, VV earns more under Option B than they ever would under Option A — permanently.

---

## 1. The Two Options on the Table

| | Option A: Full Fee + Lower Share | Option B: Discounted Fee + Higher Share |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 Build | $12,000–$20,000 | $6,000–$10,000 |
| Phase 2 Build | $25,000–$45,000 | $15,000–$25,000 |
| **Total Build Fee** | **$37,000–$65,000** | **$21,000–$35,000** |
| Revenue Share | 20–25% | 35–40% |
| Maintenance included | 10 hrs/mo | 10 hrs/mo |

**Fee discount under Option B:** $16,000–$30,000 less upfront.

## 2. The Financial Case: One-Time Fee vs. Revenue Share

| 5-year total | $37,000 | $65,000 |

This is the ceiling. If SeedLink reaches 500 subscribers ($4.18M ARR), VV's total compensation for building the asset that created that value: $37K–$65K. That's 0.2–0.3% of the value VV helped create.

| 25 | $9,925 | $3,722 | $44,663 | $223,313 |

### Breakeven on the Fee Discount

VV "gives up" $16K–$30K in build fees by choosing Option B over Option A. At 50 subscribers:
- Monthly revenue share difference (37.5% vs 22.5%): ~$2,978/mo
- **Discount recovered in ~4 months**
- Everything after month 4 is pure upside vs. Option A

## 3. Industry Standards: What Builders Typically Get

| Builder Role | Typical Revenue Share |
|---|---|
| Technical co-founder (equity equivalent) | 30–50% |
| Agency builds + maintains SaaS platform | 20–40% |
| Agency builds only, client operates | 10–20% |
| White-label reseller / channel partner | 20–30% |
| Referral / affiliate partner (no build work) | 15–30% recurring |
| Performance-based agency | 5–10% |

**Where VV sits:** VV is building the entire technical product, designing the architecture, engineering all prompts, and maintaining the platform. This is functionally a technical co-founder role. The proposed 35–40% is appropriate — it's actually conservative compared to a true technical co-founder equity split (which would be 40–50%).

**Key benchmark:** If a company that merely *refers* customers earns 30–40% recurring commission (standard in AI SaaS partner programs), a company that *builds the entire platform* should earn at least that much.

## 4. Seven Arguments for Revenue Share

### Argument 1: Incentive Alignment

Fixed-fee = VV is incentivized to finish fast and move on. Revenue share = VV is incentivized to make the platform excellent, reduce churn, optimize conversion, and proactively improve the product. Every improvement that increases subscriber count directly increases VV's income.

### Argument 2: Compensation for Ongoing Value Creation

VV is creating the core IP that generates every dollar of SaaS revenue: the AI workflow engine, prompt engineering, automation logic, white-label infrastructure. These aren't one-time deliverables — they create compounding value every month a subscriber pays. A songwriter doesn't accept a one-time fee for a song that generates streaming revenue for decades. The same principle applies.

### Argument 3: Below-Market Build Fees

Custom SaaS MVP development typically costs $50K–$300K. VV is building for $12K–$45K — effectively subsidizing the build by $30K–$280K vs. market rates. When a technical co-founder accepts below-market salary, they receive equity. Revenue share is the contractual equivalent.

### Argument 4: The Platform Cannot Exist Without the Builder

SeedLink cannot sell a SaaS product without someone building it. The platform IS the product. Without VV's execution, SeedLink has pricing tiers and a landing page but no actual software. This leverage position — creating 100% of the sellable product — justifies significant ongoing participation.

### Argument 5: Maintenance Value Alone Justifies It

A reasonable maintenance retainer for a SaaS platform of this complexity: $3,000–$8,000/mo ($36K–$96K/year). That alone equals or exceeds the entire one-time build fee. Revenue share captures maintenance value AND growth upside in one clean structure.

### Argument 6: The Valuation Multiplier

SaaS companies trade at 3–7x ARR. One-time service revenue: 1–2x EBITDA. Revenue share creates an asset with a theoretical value 10–40x higher than the one-time fee:

| Scenario | VV Annual Share | Value at 3x | Value at 5x |
|----------|----------------|-------------|-------------|
| 50 subscribers | $89,325 | $267,975 | $446,625 |
| 100 subscribers | $178,650 | $535,950 | $893,250 |
| 200 subscribers | $357,300 | $1,071,900 | $1,786,500 |

A contractual revenue share stream is itself a sellable asset. VV could hypothetically sell or securitize it.

### Argument 7: It Benefits SeedLink Too

- Lower upfront capital requirement (preserves runway)
- Gets a builder who is a true partner, not a vendor
- VV is financially motivated to make the platform succeed
- Lower risk: if the platform fails to gain traction, SeedLink paid less upfront

## 5. Risks of NOT Having Revenue Share

### Risk 1: Build-and-Walk

The predictable sequence:
1. VV builds platform at below-market rates
2. SeedLink launches and generates recurring revenue
3. SeedLink hires a cheaper developer for maintenance
4. Relationship terminated
5. VV has $0 ongoing income and $0 contractual rights

Without revenue share, VV has zero hooks after final payment. The code is delivered, the invoice is paid, and the relationship can be terminated unilaterally.

### Risk 2: Zero Upside Participation

If SeedLink reaches $4.18M ARR (500 subscribers), VV's total compensation under build-and-give: $37K–$65K. Under 35% revenue share: $1.46M/year. The gap between participating in upside and not is the gap between a project fee and a seven-figure income stream.

### Risk 3: IP Leverage Disappears

Once code is delivered and paid under a fixed fee, VV loses all leverage. Revenue share keeps VV at the table with ongoing contractual rights to the revenue stream, creating mutual dependency.

### Risk 4: Quality Incentive Decay

Fixed-fee projects incentivize getting to "done" as fast as possible. Revenue share inverts this: excellence pays, because a better product = more subscribers = more revenue for both parties.

---

## 6. Critical Contractual Protections

Revenue share only works with proper contractual safeguards:

| Protection | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Background IP carveout** | VV retains ownership of pre-existing frameworks, tools, workflow templates, and prompt engineering IP. SeedLink gets a license, not ownership. |
| **Revenue definition + audit rights** | Revenue = gross subscriber revenue. VV gets direct Stripe dashboard access to verify payments. |
| **Perpetual term with buyout clause** | Revenue share lasts as long as platform generates revenue. Buyout = 3–5x trailing 12-month payments. |
| **Minimum term (24–36 months)** | Prevents SeedLink from launching with VV's platform, then terminating and rebuilding cheaply. |
| **Source code + license linkage** | If revenue share payments stop (outside buyout), VV's IP license terminates. Mutual dependency. |
| **Anti-dilution on scope** | New tiers or products built on VV's architecture are included in the revenue share calculation. |

## 7. Comparison with GoHighLevel Model

The most directly comparable precedent is GoHighLevel — a white-label marketing automation platform:

- GoHighLevel built the platform; agencies resell under their own brands
- Agencies pay $497/mo for access, resell at $297–$497/mo per client
- Agency margins: 40–85% per client
- Documented: agencies reaching $28K–$67K MRR within 8–18 months
- GoHighLevel revenue: est. $80M+/year

**Parallel to SeedLink:** SeedLink would be the GoHighLevel equivalent — the platform owner. VV's revenue share is analogous to GoHighLevel's platform fee, except VV also built the entire product. If anything, VV's 35–40% share is more justified than GoHighLevel's platform margin because VV created the underlying technology.

## 8. Recommendation

**Choose Option B: Discounted build fee ($21K–$35K) + 35–40% revenue share.**

| Factor | Option A | Option B | Build-and-Give |
|--------|----------|----------|----------------|
| Upfront cash | $37K–$65K | $21K–$35K | $37K–$65K |
| Year 1 income (at 25 subs) | $37K–$65K + $24K | $21K–$35K + $45K | $37K–$65K |
| Year 3 income (at 100 subs) | $48K/yr | $178K/yr | $0/yr |
| 5-year total (at 100 subs) | $65K + $240K = $305K | $35K + $893K = $928K | $65K |
| Alignment incentive | Moderate | Strong | None |
| Contractual protection | Moderate | Strong | None |
| Upside at scale | Capped | Uncapped | Zero |

**The fee discount is not a concession — it's an investment.** VV invests $16K–$30K in reduced fees and receives an asset (the revenue share stream) worth $268K–$1.8M+ depending on traction.

The one non-negotiable: the contract must include the protections in Section 6. Revenue share without safeguards is a handshake deal, and handshake deals favor whoever has less to lose.
