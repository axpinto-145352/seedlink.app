## 2. Partnership Model — SMB Fit Analysis

### Does Modular Buying Match How SMBs Buy?

**Yes — strongly.** SMBs buy solutions to specific problems, not platforms. The modular approach maps directly to how SMBs think:

| SMB Problem | Module That Solves It | Typical Entry Point |
|-------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| "I need more leads" | LinkedIn Outreach (A) | $1,500-$2,200 |
| "I need to post content consistently" | Content Creation (B) | $1,500-$2,200 |
| "I need to nurture leads that aren't ready" | Email Automation (C) | $1,500-$2,200 |
| "I need to rank on Google" | SEO/GEO (D) | $1,500-$2,200 |

SMBs do not buy "full marketing automation stacks" on day one. They buy one solution, see results, then expand. The modular catalog with bundle pricing ($3,200 for 2 modules, $6,800 for 3) creates a natural expansion path.

**Key validation**: The Mavera proposal demonstrates this perfectly — recommending LinkedIn first, then Content, then SEO, then Email. That sequence mirrors how SMBs actually make purchasing decisions.

### Price Sensitivity: Is $1,500-$3,000 the Right Range?

| Price Point | SMB Reaction | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **$750-$1,000 (Paid Assessment)** | Easy yes. Low-risk entry. | Recommended in Executive Report as door-opener |
| **$1,500 (Basic)** | Competitive vs. freelancers ($500-$3,000), massively below agencies ($3,000-$7,500) | SMB pricing analysis shows Basic at market floor |
| **$2,200 (Standard)** | Sweet spot — still below agency, covers real scope | Margin analysis shows Standard is where business model works |
| **$3,000 (Premium)** | Top of SMB comfort zone for a one-time purchase without a sales call | Above this, most SMBs want a conversation first |

**Verdict**: The $1,500-$3,000 range is correct for the SMB market. The risk is that Basic ($1,500) is underpriced for non-LinkedIn modules (effective rate drops to $26-$39/hr after the 85/15 split). The pricing analysis is clear: **push buyers to Standard ($2,200) where margins are healthy.**

### How Do SMBs Prefer to Buy?

| Purchase Range | Self-Serve Online | Sales Conversation | Split |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Under $500 | 85% | 15% | Self-serve dominates |
| $500-$1,500 | 60% | 40% | Self-serve still leads |
| **$1,500-$3,000** | **35-45%** | **55-65%** | **Sales conversation preferred but not required** |
| $3,000-$10,000 | 15% | 85% | Sales conversation required |
| Over $10,000 | 5% | 95% | Enterprise sales process |

At the $1,500-$3,000 range, SMBs are split. Some will buy online if trust signals are strong (case studies, demos, guarantees). Others want a 15-minute call first. **The SeedLink model should support both paths**: Stripe checkout for confident buyers, Calendly booking for hesitant ones.

### Typical SMB Buying Journey for Automation Services

```
Week 1:  Pain recognition ("I'm spending too much on outreach / content is inconsistent")
   |
Week 2:  Google search or peer referral → discovers SeedLink
   |
Week 3:  Reads landing page, checks pricing, looks for case studies
   |
Week 4:  Either (a) books a call, or (b) buys Basic/Standard directly
   |
Week 5:  Completes onboarding questionnaire
   |
Week 7-8:  Receives build, does handoff call
   |
Month 3:  Sees results → considers adding second module
   |
Month 6:  Becomes multi-module client or converts to Lite Support
```

**Average time from awareness to purchase: 2-4 weeks.** Shorter for referrals (1-2 weeks), longer for cold inbound (4-6 weeks).

## 6. Risk Analysis

### SMB Churn Rates for Automation Services

| Service Type | Monthly Churn Rate | Average Lifetime | Lifetime Value (at $300/mo) |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| SaaS tools (self-serve) | 3-5% | 20-33 months | $6,000-$9,900 |
| Managed services (agency-like) | 5-8% | 12-20 months | $3,600-$6,000 |
| **Lite Support (projected)** | **5-8%** | **12-20 months** | **$3,600-$6,000** |
| One-time builds (no retention) | N/A | N/A | $1,500-$3,000 (one-time only) |

**Risk**: If Lite Support conversion is below 50% or churn exceeds 8%/month, recurring revenue underperforms projections. At 30% conversion and 10% churn, Lite Support revenue at Month 12 drops from $5,400/mo to ~$2,200/mo.

### Price Sensitivity to Third-Party Tool Costs

This is a material risk. The SMB's total cost is not just the build fee:

| Cost Component | Amount | Who Pays | SMB Perception |
|---------------|--------|----------|---------------|
| Build fee (one-time) | $1,500-$3,000 | Client via SeedLink | "I'm investing in automation" |
| Third-party tools (ongoing) | $110-$165/mo | Client directly | "Wait, I have MORE monthly costs?" |
| Lite Support (optional) | $250-$350/mo | Client via SeedLink | "That's another subscription" |
| **Total Year 1 cost** | **$3,120-$5,580** | | "That's still way less than an agency" |

**The $110-$165/mo tool cost is the friction point.** SMBs who buy a $1,500 build expecting zero ongoing costs will be frustrated. Mitigation: make tool costs crystal clear pre-purchase (the Mavera proposal does this well), and frame the total cost against the alternative ($42,000/year for agency LinkedIn alone).

### Capacity Constraints at VV

| Build Volume | VV Hours/Month | VV Status | Revenue (Gross) | Revenue to VV (85%) |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|----------------|---------------------|
| 2 builds/mo | 80-110 hrs | Comfortable | $4,400 | $3,740 |
| 3 builds/mo | 120-165 hrs | Manageable | $6,600 | $5,610 |
| 4 builds/mo | 160-220 hrs | At capacity | $8,800 | $7,480 |
| 5 builds/mo | 200-275 hrs | Over capacity | $11,000 | $9,350 |
| 6 builds/mo | 240-330 hrs | Requires additional resources | $15,000 | $12,750 |

**The bottleneck hits at 4 builds/month.** At 5+, VV needs to either subcontract or SeedLink needs to add delivery partners. The Partnership Agreement addresses this (Section 1.10), but the operational plan for scaling past 4/month is not yet defined.

**Template reuse mitigates partially**: The Executive Report estimates 60-70% time reduction after the first 3 builds per module. If validated, a Standard LinkedIn build drops from 40-55 hours to 16-22 hours, effectively doubling capacity.

### Competition from AI-Native Platforms

| Platform | Threat Level | Overlap with SeedLink | Mitigation |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Jasper.ai** | Medium | Content generation only — no pipeline, no outreach, no scheduling | SeedLink's integrated pipeline is the differentiator |
| **Waldium (YC S23)** | Low-Medium | Blog/docs content with AEO — no social, no outreach | Different target market (developer tools). Monitor for expansion. |
| **HubSpot Marketing Hub** | Low (for SMBs) | Full marketing automation | $800+/mo prices it out of most SMBs. SeedLink is 10x cheaper. |
| **Apollo.io + Instantly.ai** | Medium | Email outreach + lead gen | These are tools in SeedLink's stack, not competitors. But SMBs may go direct. |

**The real competition is not platforms — it's inertia.** Most SMBs who need automation don't buy it. The sales challenge is convincing them to act, not beating a competitor.
